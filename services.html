Services · The Employee Lifecycle

From the first impression to the final conversation — every stage matters.

The way you attract, develop, and retain your people at every stage determines whether you build a company worth staying at. Here's where Thrive enters — and what changes when we do.

Where Thrive Enters

Every stage. Every moment that shapes the experience.

Tap the dollar sign on any card to see what it costs to leave that stage unaddressed.

Employer Brand

"What do people say about you when you're not in the room?"

Your employer brand exists whether you've built it or not. At 30–150 employees, one authentic culture story travels faster than any job ad.

Thrive defines your Employee Value Proposition and ensures every touchpoint — job descriptions, interview experience, public presence — tells the same story.
Companies with a weak employer brand pay up to 10% more per hire. At 15 hires/year averaging $70K, that's $105K in avoidable salary premium — before recruiting fees.
Recruitment

"Are you hiring for who you are — or who you used to be?"

Thrive doesn't source candidates. The interview is the first culture experience a candidate has — it should be designed, not improvised.

Thrive aligns your recruiting process to your culture — values and behavior-based interview design, structured debrief guides, and job descriptions built around what actually predicts success in your environment.
A structured interview process and strong employer brand reduce cost-per-hire by up to 50% and early attrition by 28%. For a 15-hire year, that's $35K–$70K saved in recruiting costs alone.
Onboarding

"They said yes. The clock starts now."

Most companies wing the first 90 days. A new hire who feels unsupported in week one doesn't wait for the annual review to decide they made a mistake.

Thrive designs the full experience — offer acceptance through 90 days — with role-specific ramp plans, manager playbooks, and culture built in from Day 1. Handed over to run internally.
1 in 3 new hires leaves within 90 days without structured onboarding. At $45K average replacement cost, losing 3 new hires in a year = $135K gone. Structured onboarding cuts early attrition by 82%.
Performance

"Does your team know what winning looks like?"

When there's no goal-setting, no feedback cadence, no accountability structure — managers avoid hard conversations and good people leave feeling invisible.

Thrive builds a performance system calibrated to your culture — goals, check-ins, review cadence, and manager training. When it's time for talent calibration and succession discussions, Thrive leads those sessions.
Disengaged employees cost 34% of their salary in lost productivity. At 50 employees with 20% disengaged, that's $238K in annual drain. Manager quality drives 70% of that variance.
Development

"Who's ready for what's next — and do they know it?"

Most growing companies rely on external hiring when the talent they need is already in the room. The best organizations invest in potential before they post a job.

Thrive identifies high-potential employees, builds succession maps, closes development gaps, and designs the Build-Buy-Borrow talent strategy — so the next leader comes from within first.
Replacing one high-performer costs 50–200% of their salary. Losing 2 key people per year at $90K with no succession plan = $90K–$360K absorbed annually — most of it preventable.
Engagement

"Is the culture you started with still the culture you have?"

Culture doesn't break all at once. It drifts — one new manager, one growth spurt, one leadership transition nobody narrated. By the time most companies notice, it's been 18 months.

Thrive runs a culture diagnostic, installs a pulse survey with a measurement dashboard, translates values into observable behaviors, and facilitates leadership alignment before drift becomes a retention crisis. Benefits and 401(k) design aligned to your culture — broker-partnered for implementation.
Companies in the top quartile for culture outperform peers by 33% in revenue growth. For a $10M business, that gap is worth $3.3M. Culture drift doesn't show up on a P&L until it's already expensive.
Offboarding

"How someone leaves shapes who wants to join."

Every exit is data. A poorly handled departure undoes months of culture work — and signals to every future candidate what working here is really like.

Thrive designs the offboarding process, builds the exit interview framework, and turns departure data into actionable insight — feeding what's learned back into employer brand and the next hire's onboarding.
A negative review from a poorly offboarded employee can reduce qualified applicant volume by 29% and increase cost-per-hire by 10% company-wide. The exit conversation costs nothing. The absence of one costs everything downstream.

Workforce Planning & Org Design — The Strategic Layer Beneath Every Stage

Scenario modeling, org design, role architecture, and HRIS selection — so every people decision connects to where the business is going. Reactive headcount decisions cost 30–40% more in people spend. Thrive brings enterprise-grade planning discipline without the enterprise complexity.
